Functional Principles

In the Liquidity module, you manage two types of budget sheets.

Operational Budget Sheet

It consists of your forecasts based on the production, excluding tax, with the forecast transaction dates. In this budget sheet, you enter your forecasts.

You can form forecasts by importing G/L data.

The import process is described in the Importing G/L Data topic.

Treasury Budget Sheet

It consists of your actual treasury at time T including all taxes, with the forecast dates of collection or disbursement.

The Treasury Budget Sheet is obtained by applying to the Operational Budget Sheet calculation formulas that you define for each budget code. In this way, collection and disbursement dates, VATs, fringe benefits, etc. are calculated.

You may also replace in the budget sheet the past forecasts by the actuals (cash flows created in Cash). You are then working on a budget called "Mixed Budget".

The discrepancy between the forecasts and the actuals is calculated with the cash flows saved in the Cash module.

So, you can perform a review of your budget based on this analysis and defer the non-actual forecasts.

You may also analyze you budget through the comparison of different budget exercises: comparing different versions of a same exercise, different entity groups, or different years.

The budget can be revised indefinitely until the exercise closure. You can view these successive budget versions at any time.

The management of budget sheets is described in the Budget Sheet Entry and Analysis topic.

Before entering the budget, you need to set up all the elements described in the Setup topic.

This setup enables the definition of your activity environment and the structure of the sheet (budget codes, calculation rules, information display...).

Once you finalized the budget, you may automatically generate the corresponding cash flows in Cash based on Treasury Budget Transactions.

Finally, you can generate reports based on these budget sheets, save these reports in several formats and send them.

The management of Reports is described in the topic Reports.
