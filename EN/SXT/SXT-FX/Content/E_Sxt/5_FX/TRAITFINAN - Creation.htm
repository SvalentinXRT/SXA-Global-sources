Creation and Modification

The following topics detail the creation and modification procedures for FX Transactions, as well as the setup of the filtering criteria in the relevant pages of the application.

Spot and Forward Exchange Transactions

For each spot and forward buy/sell transaction, two flows are generated in the Treasury.

Example: A company buys USD 10.000 for which it will have to pay the counterparty in euros, its local currency. Settlement accounts are entered.

Two cash flows are assigned to this transaction type:

  • a receipt flow in the main currency (dollars purchase),
  • a disbursement flow in the countervalue currency (sale of euros in exchange for dollars).
 Swap

Swaps are combinations of two transactions with opposite directions: a spot or forward exchange transaction (first leg) with a forward exchange transaction (second leg). For each currency swap, four flows are generated in the Treasury.

First leg (buy)

  • A receipt flow in the main currency (dollars purchase)
  • A disbursement flow in the countervalue currency (sale of euros in exchange for dollars)

Second leg (sell)

  • A disbursement flow in the main currency (dollars purchase)
  • A receipt flow in the countervalue currency (sale of euros in exchange for dollars)
The deletion of an FX transaction involves the automatic deletion of the associated cash flows.

FX Transactions can have two statuses: open and closed (when they are settled).

When creating transactions, remember that in , a purchase of currency corresponds to a receipt in the transaction main currency (foreign currency) and to a disbursement in the countervalue currency (local currency).
