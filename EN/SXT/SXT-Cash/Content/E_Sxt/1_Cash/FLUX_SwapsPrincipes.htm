Principles

For borrowing or investment, the swap‘s interest lies in the transformation of the interest rate structure.

Swaps are mainly used to manage exposure to rate risk, for instance, by turning floating-rate credit into a fixed-rate credit and conversely.

In the same way, for asset-liability management, rate swaps enable the structures balancing between principal uses and resources.

The strictly identical notional principals are not exchanged (otherwise this would mean paying and receiving the same amount!).

The interest flows which are different, are exchanged.

Throughout the swap duration, each counterparty:

  • Must periodically pay the interests for the leg in which it borrows.
  • Must receive in exchange the interests for the leg in which it lends.

In some swaps the notional principals are actually exchanged, for example in currency swaps where the principals are in two different currencies.

The Rate Swap is associated with an underlying.

Rates Swapped

Fixed Rate: It is specified at transaction agreement, depending on the market conditions, the characteristics of the underlying you want to hedge (Duration, Payment Dates, Principal), and the interest calculation basis.

Floating Rate: It is periodically set and corresponds to the interest calculation basis.

 Principal and Currency

The principal is the amount on which interests are paid. It may vary during the transaction life cycle according to a specified scheme, such as for Amortized Swaps.

For a single-currency rate swap, where both legs have the same principal and currency, there is no principal payment at the beginning, nor at the end of the transaction.

Regarding currency swaps managed in FX module, there is an exchange of principal at the beginning and at the end of the transaction.

The swap’s currency is the one used for the payments.

Vanilla swaps are single-currency swaps.

Multi-currency swaps also exist, for instance: quantos.

 Duration and Schedule

Swaps last between 1 month and 20 years.

They can be terminated at any time as of the transaction start, on two conditions:

  • Both parties agree.
  • Termination cash is paid by one of the parties, corresponding to the swap replacement value in market conditions.

The following elements are precisely specified when entering the swap contract:

  • Contract Date
  • Value Date
  • Frequency (Monthly, Quarterly, Biannually or Annually)
  • Reset Dates for the floating rate
  • Payment Dates
  • Maturity Date
 Swap Types

The swaps with an asterisk are managed by the application.

 
Type

Definition
Vanilla Swap*

Exchange of fixed-rate interests flows against floating-rate interests flows.
Basis Swap*

Exchange of floating-rate interests flows on two different rate indices.
Forward Swap

Swap that starts on a set date in the future.
Amortized Swap

Swap whose principal is amortized over time like its underlying.
Zero-coupon

Exchange of fixed against floating, or fixed against fixed flows. The flows related to the fixed rate are generated only once at the beginning or at the end.
Asset Swap

Swap where paid flows are turned into an asset that one of the parties holds in its portfolio.
Quanto or Differential

Exchange of interest cash flows at floating rate against interest payments at fixed rate, both expressed in the same currency, whereas the rate references come from two different currencies.
